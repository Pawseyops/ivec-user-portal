Applications are invited to access Pawsey-funded iVEC infrastructure for 2011. Contained within this web site is the process for application. If you require any assistance completing this application, please contact help@ivec.org.

Background

The Pawsey project is an $80 million Commonwealth Super Science funded infrastructure project to put a petascale supercomputing system in Perth that will be managed by iVEC, and operated through funds from the Western Australian State Government and the iVEC Partners. The allocation of time on this Pawsey-funded infrastructure is determined through mechanisms agreed by iVEC and the Commonwealth.

The agreed allocations for July 1 – December 31, 2011 are:

  • 25% Radio Astronomy
  • 25% Geosciences
  • 30% iVEC Partners
  • 15% National Merit Allocation Scheme
  • 5% Director's Discretionary Allocation

These allocations may change in future years. Half of the machine has been initially allocated to radio astronomy and the geosciences, both of which are priority areas for the State and Commonwealth governments. The radio astronomy share is predominantly for the operational requirements of facilities like ASKAP, MWA, and DIRP. The Partner share is available only to researchers from any of the iVEC Partners (i.e. CSIRO and the four WA public universities), whereas the National Merit Allocation Scheme will be available to researchers Australia-wide. Finally, the Director's share is to support pilot or startup projects, code scaling work, urgent computing requirements and large-scale data intensive visualisation.

Although access to the (non-Director's Discretionary) shares will ultimately be via different mechanisms, in this first round, which covers the 2nd half of 2011, a single form will be used for all four. Please note that time is awarded to projects, which may have many users.

Infrastructure

Compute resources in this first round of time allocation are available on epic.ivec.org, a 85 TF system comprised of 9,600 cores with 500 terabytes of high-performance storage housed at iVEC@Murdoch.

Who should complete this application?

As iVEC moves forwards over the next four years, all of its supercomputing infrastructure will be funded through the Pawsey Project. This will be a big change to the iVEC community as we are now required to allocate time through a much more formal process than in the past. Consequently, this form may be unexpected or even appear intimidating to many users. However, there is always help at hand, so please don't hesitate to contact help@ivec.org with any questions. Please make every effort to answer all sections of the form. We understand that this is a learning process for both iVEC and our users and so this first round will be treated accordingly.

The applications will be evaluated by a mixture of science and supercomputing experts, so please target your application to an audience with a generic scientific background. Do not assume they have deep knowledge of your application area.

Note that time is allocated to projects - these can have many team members. Thus, applications are submitted by Project Leaders on behalf of the project team. Members of a project team are not require to complete this application form, however they must still agree to the iVEC conditions of use at http://www.ivec.org/services/apply-account/condition-use. Typically Project Leaders will be research leaders within a scientific research group, laboratory, or community.

The Project Leader is responsible for

  • Completing this application form
  • Being the primary point of contact between the project team members and iVEC
  • Completing the project reports within 30 days at the end of the granting period
  • Submitting requests to add/remove users to existing projects
  • Management of data storage and sharing during and after the project

Please note that iVEC will manage the allocation on a best effort basis but cannot guarantee that approved projects will receive their full allocations and that unused allocations will not roll over to the next allocation period.
